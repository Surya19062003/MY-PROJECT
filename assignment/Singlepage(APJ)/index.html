Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Introduction:

  APJ Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Dr. Kalam was a renowned aerospace engineer and played a key role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He was often referred to as the "People's President" due to his affable and accessible nature. Before his presidency, Kalam worked with India's space research organization, ISRO, and the Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). He was a strong advocate for education, particularly in the fields of science and technology, and often interacted with students to inspire and motivate them. Dr. Kalam was a recipient of numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, and he remains an inspirational figure for his contributions to science, education, and national development.

  From 1992 to 1997 Kalam was scientific adviser to the defense minister, and he later served as principal scientific adviser (1999–2001) to the government with the rank of cabinet minister. His prominent role in the country’s 1998 nuclear weapons tests solidified India as a nuclear power and established Kalam as a national hero, although the tests caused great concern in the international community. In 1998 Kalam put forward a countrywide plan called Technology Vision 2020, which he described as a road map for transforming India from a less-developed to a developed society in 20 years. The plan called for, among other measures, increasing agricultural productivity, emphasizing technology as a vehicle for economic growth, and widening access to health care and education.

  In 2002 India’s ruling National Democratic Alliance (NDA) put forward Kalam to succeed outgoing President Kocheril Raman Narayanan. Kalam was nominated by the Hindu nationalist (Hindutva) NDA even though he was Muslim, and his stature and popular appeal were such that even the main opposition party, the Indian National Congress, also proposed his candidacy. Kalam easily won the election and was sworn in as India’s 11th president, a largely ceremonial post, in July 2002. He left office at the end of his term in 2007 and was succeeded by Pratibha Patil, the country’s first woman president. Upon returning to civilian life, Kalam remained committed to using science and technology to transform India into a developed country and served as a lecturer at several universities. On July 27, 2015, he collapsed while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong and was pronounced dead from cardiac arrest soon afterward. Kalam wrote several books, including an autobiography, Wings of Fire (1999).

BIOGRAPHY

Early Life:

  In addition to his academic pursuits, APJ Abdul Kalam was deeply influenced by spiritual and ethical values. His father, Jainulabdeen, was not only a devout Muslim but also a person of great integrity and kindness. Kalam imbibed these values, and throughout his life, he remained committed to principles of honesty, humility, and selfless service. Kalam's journey into the field of aeronautics began with his admission to the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) in 1955. He specialized in aeronautical engineering and, in 1960, graduated with honors. Following his graduation, Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist. His initial years were dedicated to research on engine technologies for missiles. One of the key milestones in Kalam's career came in the 1980s when he played a pivotal role in the development of India's indigenous satellite launch vehicle program. His leadership in the successful development of the SLV (Satellite Launch Vehicle) and the PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle) significantly bolstered India's capabilities in space technology. Another significant chapter in Kalam's career unfolded during the 1990s when he led the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. These achievements earned him the nickname "Missile Man of India." His contributions to India's defense capabilities were widely acknowledged, and he became a respected figure in the scientific community. Apart from his scientific endeavors, Kalam was a passionate advocate for education. He believed that education was the key to unlocking the potential of individuals and the nation. Even during his presidency, he continued to engage with students and youth, inspiring them to pursue excellence in science and technology. Kalam's popularity extended beyond the scientific and academic realms. His simple lifestyle, accessible demeanor, and motivational speeches endeared him to people from all walks of life. After his term as the President of India, Kalam continued to be actively involved in educational and philanthropic activities until his passing in 2015. His legacy lives on, and he is remembered not only for his scientific contributions but also for being a role model and source of inspiration for generations.

Career:

  APJ Abdul Kalam's illustrious career spanned several decades and encompassed various roles in the fields of science, technology, and public service. Here is an overview of his notable career milestones:

1. Aeronautical Engineer (1958-1982): After completing his education at the Madras Institute of Technology, Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist in 1958. His early work focused on the development of indigenous guided missiles. Kalam played a crucial role in projects like the design and development of a hovercraft and the country's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle (SLV).

2. Indian Space Research Organization (1969-1982): In 1969, Kalam moved to the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), where he served as the project director for the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) that successfully deployed the Rohini satellite into space in 1980. This marked a significant milestone in India's space program.

3.Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (1982-1999): Kalam returned to DRDO in 1982 to lead the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP). Under his leadership, India achieved major breakthroughs in missile technology, including the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. Kalam's expertise earned him the nickname "Missile Man of India."

4. Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister (1992-1999): Kalam served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India and also as the Secretary of the Defense Research and Development Organization. During this period, India conducted its nuclear tests in Pokhran in 1998, which further established the country's capabilities in defense technology.

5.President of India (2002-2007): In 2002, Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India. His presidency was marked by his commitment to education, youth empowerment, and his vision for a developed India. Kalam was the first scientist and bachelor to hold the office of the President.

Post-Presidency (2007-2015): After serving as the President, Kalam continued to be an influential figure. He remained actively involved in educational and inspirational activities, engaging with students and young people across the country. Kalam also authored several books, sharing his thoughts on a wide range of topics, including leadership, education, and India's development. Throughout his career, APJ Abdul Kalam's dedication to scientific research, innovation, and his commitment to the betterment of society left an indelible mark on India's technological landscape and its youth. His legacy continues to inspire generations of Indians.

Education:

APJ Abdul Kalam's educational journey reflects his strong commitment to learning and his passion for aeronautics. Here is an overview of his educational background:

1. Early Education: Kalam's early education took place in Rameswaram, his hometown in Tamil Nadu. He attended the Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram. Despite coming from a modest background, his parents instilled in him the value of education and supported his academic pursuits.

2. Bachelor's Degree in Physics (1954): Kalam graduated from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, affiliated with the University of Madras, in 1954. He completed his bachelor's degree in physics.

3. Aeronautical Engineering at MIT (1955-1960): Kalam's interest in aeronautics led him to pursue a degree in aeronautical engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) in Chennai. He enrolled in 1955 and graduated in 1960.

4. Doctorate in Aerospace Engineering (1958): While working at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) in 1958, Kalam began his doctoral studies. In 1962, he completed his Doctorate of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Aerospace Engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in the United States. His thesis was titled "Design and Development of a Hovercraft."

Kalam's educational journey laid the foundation for his subsequent career in aerospace engineering and scientific research. Throughout his life, he emphasized the importance of education, particularly in science and technology, as a means to empower individuals and contribute to national development. Even as President, he continued to engage with students and educators, encouraging them to pursue knowledge and innovation.

ARCHIVEMENTS

Scientific Achievements:

APJ Abdul Kalam was a highly accomplished scientist with significant contributions to India's space and defense programs. His scientific achievements are notable for their impact on the country's technological advancements. Here are some key highlights:

1. Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV): In the 1970s, Kalam played a crucial role in the development of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV). The successful launch of the Rohini satellite in 1980 marked a significant milestone for India's space program.

2. Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP): Kalam spearheaded the IGMDP, an initiative aimed at developing a comprehensive range of guided missiles. Under his leadership, India achieved significant milestones, including the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. The successful test firing of these missiles demonstrated India's capability in missile technology.

3. Agni Missile Series: Kalam played a pivotal role in the development of the Agni series of ballistic missiles, designed for long-range strategic purposes. The Agni missiles contributed to India's deterrence capabilities and marked a technological leap in the field of missile development.

4. Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests: As the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the Defense Research and Development Organization, Kalam played a key role in India's nuclear weapons program. The successful nuclear tests at Pokhran in 1998 established India as a nuclear weapons state.

5. Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV):Kalam's leadership and vision were instrumental in the successful development and operationalization of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV). PSLV became a workhorse for India's space agency, ISRO, enabling the country to launch satellites into various orbits with precision.

6. Ballistic Missile Defense (BMD) System: Kalam was actively involved in the development of India's Ballistic Missile Defense System. This system aims to provide protection against incoming ballistic missile threats and further enhances India's defense capabilities.

Kalam's scientific achievements earned him recognition and accolades both nationally and internationally. His role as the "Missile Man of India" and his contributions to space and defense technology have left a lasting legacy, showcasing India's prowess in scientific and technological advancements.

Presidency:

APJ Abdul Kalam served as the 11th President of India from July 25, 2002, to July 25, 2007. His presidency was marked by a unique blend of scientific acumen, humility, and a deep connection with the youth of the nation. Here are some key aspects of his presidency: People's President: Kalam was affectionately known as the "People's President" due to his approachable and personable nature. Despite holding a high constitutional office, he maintained a simple and humble lifestyle, making him immensely popular among the citizens of India. Focus on Education and Youth Empowerment: Throughout his term, Kalam continued to emphasize the importance of education and aimed to inspire the youth. He often visited educational institutions, engaging with students and encouraging them to pursue careers in science and technology. His interactions with students earned him the reputation of being a motivational figure. Vision for a Developed India: Kalam had a vision for India to become a developed nation by the year 2020. He articulated his vision in the book titled "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium," where he outlined his ideas for transforming India into a knowledge-based society with a strong economy. Inclusive Presidency: Kalam's presidency was characterized by his inclusiveness and efforts to reach out to diverse sections of society. He focused on bridging the gap between urban and rural areas, encouraging development that benefited all citizens. Advocacy for Technology and Innovation: Given his background as a scientist, Kalam continued to champion the cause of technology and innovation during his presidency. He believed that technological advancements were crucial for the progress of the nation. Decision on Dissolution of Bihar Assembly: One notable event during Kalam's presidency was his decision on the dissolution of the Bihar Legislative Assembly in 2005. This decision, taken on the advice of the Union Cabinet, marked a rare instance of the President exercising discretionary powers in matters related to state governance. Post-Presidential Contributions: After completing his term, Kalam did not retire from public life. Instead, he continued to be actively involved in various educational and inspirational initiatives, promoting scientific thinking, and motivating the youth. APJ Abdul Kalam's presidency left an indelible mark on the hearts of the Indian people. His leadership style, vision for the nation, and dedication to education continue to inspire generations.

Awards and Honors:

APJ Abdul Kalam received numerous awards and honors in recognition of his contributions to science, technology, and public service. Some of the notable awards and distinctions conferred upon him include:
1. Padma Bhushan (1981): Kalam was awarded the Padma Bhushan, the third-highest civilian award in India, in recognition of his exceptional service to the country in the field of science and engineering.
2. Padma Vibhushan (1990): He received the Padma Vibhushan, the second-highest civilian award in India, for his outstanding contributions to the scientific community, particularly in the field of defense technology.
3. Bharat Ratna (1997): APJ Abdul Kalam was honored with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, for his distinguished service to the nation as a scientist and his pivotal role in the development of India's space and defense capabilities.
4. Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration (1997): Kalam received this award for promoting and fostering national integration in India through his work in science and technology.
5. Veer Savarkar Award (1998): He was conferred with the Veer Savarkar Award in the field of public administration for his exceptional leadership and contributions.
6. International von Kármán Wings Award (2009): The California Institute of Technology and the International Academy of Astronautics honored Kalam with the von Kármán Wings Award for his outstanding contributions to the field of aeronautics and astronautics.
7. Honorary Doctorates: Kalam received honorary doctorates from various universities around the world in recognition of his achievements and contributions to science, education, and public service.
8. IEEE Honorary Membership (2011): The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) conferred honorary membership on Kalam for his leadership in the development of India's space technology.
9. Doctor of Laws (Honoris Causa) from Simon Fraser University (2012): He was awarded an honorary Doctor of Laws degree by Simon Fraser University in Canada for his contributions to science and humanity.
These awards and honors reflect the widespread recognition and respect for APJ Abdul Kalam's multifaceted contributions, spanning science, technology, education, and national development.

LEGACY

APJ Abdul Kalam left behind a powerful and enduring legacy that continues to inspire people in India and around the world. His legacy encompasses various aspects, including science and technology, education, leadership, and values. Here are some key facets of Dr. Kalam's legacy:

  1. Inspiration to Youth: Kalam was a source of inspiration for countless young people. His life story, marked by humble beginnings and exceptional achievements, motivated individuals to dream big and strive for excellence. He often spoke directly to students, encouraging them to pursue education, embrace innovation, and contribute to the progress of the nation.
  2. Missile Man of India: Kalam's contributions to India's missile and defense programs earned him the title of the "Missile Man of India." His leadership and vision played a pivotal role in establishing India as a capable player in missile technology, and he remains an icon in the field of aerospace engineering.
  3. People's President: Kalam's presidency was characterized by his accessibility, simplicity, and a genuine concern for the well-being of the people. His approach to the presidency earned him the affectionate title of the "People's President," and he remains a symbol of humility and public service.
  4. Advocate for Education: Throughout his life, Kalam was a passionate advocate for education. He believed that education had the power to transform individuals and nations. His emphasis on science education, research, and the development of a scientific temper left a lasting impact on educational policies and initiatives in India.
  5. Vision 2020: Kalam's vision for India to become a developed nation by the year 2020 continues to guide discussions on national development. His ideas, outlined in the book "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium," serve as a roadmap for policymakers and thinkers aspiring for a prosperous and technologically advanced India.
  6. Spirit of Innovation: Kalam's emphasis on innovation and creativity, particularly among the youth, continues to shape conversations on entrepreneurship and technology in India. He believed in the potential of young minds to drive innovation and contribute to the country's progress.
  7. Philosophy of Leadership: Kalam's leadership style, characterized by humility, integrity, and a focus on collective success, has left a lasting impact. His philosophy of leadership emphasizes the importance of empowering and inspiring others to achieve common goals.
  8. Author and Thought Leader: Kalam authored several books, sharing his thoughts on a wide range of topics, including science, education, leadership, and spirituality. His writings continue to be a source of wisdom and guidance for those seeking inspiration and knowledge.
  9. National Mourning: Kalam's passing in 2015 led to a national outpouring of grief. The respect and admiration he earned were evident in the widespread mourning across the country, and his funeral was attended by a diverse range of people, including political leaders, scientists, students, and common citizens.

In summary, APJ Abdul Kalam's legacy is multifaceted and enduring, encompassing his contributions to science, education, and leadership. His life and teachings continue to shape the aspirations and values of individuals, particularly the youth, and his impact on India's progress remains significant.
